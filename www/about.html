The acronym ARCHIE stands for Another Remote Community Hotspot for Instruction and Education. The word another is included in the acronym to acknowledge that it was inspired by other projects, particularly the RACHEL Pi project.

The ARCHIE Pi should run on all recent versions of the Raspberry Pi and includes friendly setup and installation scripts. The server runs on top of a standard Raspberry Pi OS Lite Linux operating system and a variety of additional open-source software packages. It also includes some tuning to reduce to probability of SD card corruption in the event of power failure. The ARCHIE Pi can make use of a wide variety of open educational resources, like the ones made available by World Possible at oer2go.org.

The ARCHIE Pi was developed by faculty and students from Calvin University.
